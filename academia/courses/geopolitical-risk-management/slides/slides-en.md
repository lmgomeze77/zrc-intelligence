<div class="label">Block 01 · 0–3 min</div>

# Geopolitical risk<br>as a price factor

---

## 24 February 2022: a lesson in pricing

<div class="timecode">00:00 → 03:00</div>

- The MOEX index (Moscow Exchange) **fell 45% in 72 hours**
- Within 10 days, the ruble lost **30% of its value** against the USD
- Funds with Russian exposure suffered losses of **−60% to −90%** in weeks
- The shock was not a geopolitical surprise — it was a *pricing* surprise

> "Markets had not priced in the war. Investors relying purely on financial models had no tools to see it coming."

<!--
Speaker note: This is the masterclass's central hook. Pause here and ask participants what they would have needed to anticipate this price move. The answer leads directly to the ZRC framework.
-->

---

## Why classical models are not enough

<div class="timecode">01:30 → 03:00</div>

Traditional financial models are calibrated on historical market data.
Geopolitical risk **breaks the statistical distribution**: it is discontinuous, non-linear,
and its correlations change under stress.

**What the classical model is missing:**

- Institutional and power analysis (who decides, with what incentives)
- Non-market signal reading (speeches, military positioning, sanctions)
- A transmission framework: through which channel does the shock reach the asset?

**The solution is not more data — it is a different type of analysis.**

<div class="label">Block 02 · 3–9 min</div>

# The ZRC Framework<br>Four layers of risk

---

## The ZRC Framework: from macro to operational

<div class="timecode">03:00 → 06:00</div>

Geopolitical risk is not a single number. It is the sum of **four layers** that operate
on different time horizons and amplify each other when they coincide.

| Layer | Name | Typical horizon |
|-------|------|----------------|
| **1** | Structural / Macro | Decades |
| **2** | Regulatory / Institutional | 1–5 years |
| **3** | Operational / Sectoral | 0–24 months |
| **4** | Market | Days to months |

> The more layers simultaneously compromised,<br>the greater the severity and the lower the reversibility of the total risk.

<!--
Speaker note: The key point is *concurrence* of layers. A single regulatory risk is manageable; four active layers simultaneously is a systemic alarm signal. Example: Turkey 2024.
-->

---

## The four layers in detail

<div class="timecode">06:00 → 09:00</div>

**Layer 1 — Structural:** Political regime, rule of law, institutional quality.
Slow-moving variables; high impact on long-term contracts and concessions.

**Layer 2 — Regulatory:** Legal framework, sanctions, investment treaties, tax policy.
Can change rapidly by decree; determines convertibility and profit repatriation.

**Layer 3 — Operational:** Armed conflict, strikes, logistics disruptions, corridor closures.
Direct impact on revenues; highest media visibility.

**Layer 4 — Market:** Sovereign CDS, credit spreads, exchange rates, risk premia.
The *thermometer* of the system: it synthesises layers 1–3 into observable prices.

<!--
Speaker note: Mental exercise to open here — at which layer did Russian risk first manifest in 2014? And in 2022? The answer (L1 in 2014, L3 precipitates in 2022) illustrates the accumulation dynamic.
-->

<div class="label">Block 03 · 9–16 min</div>

# Transmission channels<br>to asset prices

---

## The four channels: from shock to price

<div class="timecode">09:00 → 12:00</div>

A geopolitical shock does not affect all assets equally.
The impact depends on the **transmission channel** and the **exposure** of each position.

| Channel | Core mechanism | Most exposed assets |
|---------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Energy** | Supply restriction → price spike | Utilities, transport, industry |
| **Supply chains** | Physical flow disruption | Manufacturing, tech, consumer |
| **Currencies & capital flows** | Flight-to-quality, FX controls | EM, exporters, USD-denominated debt |
| **Risk premia** | Broad re-rating of asset/sector | All asset classes; affects multiples |

<!--
Speaker note: The risk premia channel is the hardest to anticipate because it acts on *all* assets in a geography, not just those directly exposed to the shock. Israeli tourism in 2023 illustrates indirect contagion.
-->

---

## Cross-reading: channel × portfolio position

<div class="timecode">12:00 → 16:00</div>

**Example: military exercises in the Taiwan Strait (hypothetical, 21 days)**

| Position | Dominant channel | Direction | Speed |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|-------|
| European automaker (TSMC chips) | Supply chain | **↓ severely negative** | Immediate |
| Trans-Pacific container shipper (HY bonds) | Risk premia + supply | **↓** | Immediate |
| Chinese rare earth fund | Supply chain | **↑** (restriction = price) | Short-term |
| Latin American copper producers | Relative risk premia | **→ relatively positive** | Immediate |

> The same crisis can be negative for one position and positive for another within the same portfolio.

# ZRC geopolitical<br>scoring methodology

---

## The ZRC formula: from layers to a number

<div class="timecode">16:00 → 19:00</div>

The **ZRC Geopolitical Score** translates qualitative analysis into a comparable metric.

**Scale: 0–100** (0 = no risk; 100 = maximum risk)

**Formula:**

```
Score = (L1×1.0 + L2×1.2 + L3×1.3 + L4×0.8) / 4.3 × 100
```

Each layer is scored from **0 to 25** with substantiated arguments.

**The weightings reflect:**
- L3 (Operational) ×1.3 → greatest immediate impact on cash flows
- L2 (Regulatory) ×1.2 → determines convertibility of returns
- L1 (Structural) ×1.0 → slow-mover; relevant over long horizons
- L4 (Market) ×0.8 → consequence, not cause; already visible in prices

<!--
Speaker note: The weighting is not universal — in a venture capital fund, L1 would carry more weight. In a short-duration bond fund, L4 would dominate. Participants should adapt weights to the asset profile.
-->

---

## The three decision thresholds

<div class="timecode">19:00 → 22:00</div>

| Score | Level | Recommended posture |
|-------|-------|---------------------|
| **0–35** | Low risk | Invest with standard due diligence |
| **36–55** | Moderate risk | Invest with conditions: hedges, SPA clauses |
| **56–75** | Elevated risk | Defer or reduce exposure; revisit thesis |
| **76–100** | Severe risk | Decline or require an extraordinary premium |

**The score does not replace judgement — it structures it.**

Thresholds are entry points into deeper qualitative analysis,
not automatic buy/sell signals.

<div class="score-box">
An asset scoring 38 can be an excellent investment with the right hedges.<br>
An asset scoring 70 may be unacceptable even at an attractive price.
</div>

# Case study<br>Red Sea logistics corridor<br>2023–2024

---

## The shock: Houthi attacks and the traffic collapse

<div class="timecode">22:00 → 24:00</div>

**From October 2023:** Iran-backed Houthi militias attack merchant vessels in the Red Sea
and the Bab-el-Mandeb Strait in retaliation for the Israeli offensive in Gaza.

**Quantified impact:**

- Suez Canal traffic: **−42%** in Q1 2024
- Spot Asia-Europe freight rates: **×3.5** in 8 weeks
- Routes diverted via Cape of Good Hope: **+14 days** and **+30% operating cost**
- Most affected sectors: European retail, consumer electronics, automotive

**Which layer triggered the shock?** L3 Operational (armed conflict in a logistics corridor)  
**Which layer amplified it?** L4 Market (repricing of freight and insurance)

---

## Applying the ZRC Framework to the Suez port asset

<div class="timecode">24:00 → 26:00</div>

**Asset: Port of Ain Sokhna (southern entrance of the Suez Canal, Egypt)**

| Layer | Score | Key driver |
|-------|-------|------------|
| L1 Structural | 15 / 25 | Critical external debt + nationalisation track record |
| L2 Regulatory | 16 / 25 | Past intervention + local participation requirements |
| L3 Operational | 19 / 25 | Direct impact of Houthi attacks; Canal closure risk |
| L4 Market | 14 / 25 | Egyptian sovereign CDS ~450 bp; EM discount |

**Weighted score:**
`(15×1.0 + 16×1.2 + 19×1.3 + 14×0.8) / 4.3 × 100` **≈ 64 / 100 → Elevated Risk**

**Posture conclusion:** Defer or demand a political risk premium in the valuation.
Revisit when Canal traffic recovers to 2023 levels with security guarantees.

# Synthesis and<br>next steps

---

## Three principles for investing under geopolitical uncertainty

<div class="timecode">26:00 → 27:00</div>

**1. Geopolitical risk has a price, even when it isn't quoted.**
Markets reprice it sharply when it materialises. Your edge is anticipating it.

**2. Not all layers matter equally — and not for all assets.**
An infrastructure fund needs to master L2 regulatory risk.
A commodities fund needs to master L3 operational risk and the energy channel.

**3. Scoring is a starting point, not an endpoint.**
A number from 0 to 100 organises the analysis; it does not replace expert judgement
on the political nuances that data can never fully capture.

> The geopolitically sophisticated investor does not predict the future.<br>They build portfolios that are robust across multiple possible scenarios.
